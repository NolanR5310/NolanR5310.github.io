Mk.gee Is In

The emergence of the artist mg.kee introduced a fresh blend of soulful guitar playing and expressive vocals, rooted in indie and alternative sounds. Their unique style and intimate songwriting quickly gained attention for its emotional depth and raw authenticity..

The Takanaka Comeback

Masayoshi Takanaka rose to popularity in the late 1970s and 1980s with his vibrant, genre-blending guitar work that combined jazz fusion, funk, and city pop. Known for his flashy performances and tropical-inspired sound, he became a key figure in Japan's music scene and later gained international recognition as city pop saw a global resurgence.

Mayer is King

John Mayer is an American singer-songwriter and guitarist known for his blend of pop-rock, blues, and soul influences. Rising to fame in the early 2000s with hits like "Your Body Is a Wonderland," he later gained critical respect for his guitar work and deeper songwriting, especially through albums like Continuum and his work with the John Mayer Trio.

In loving Memory of Jeff Buckley

Jeff Buckley was a profoundly talented singer-songwriter known for his soaring vocal range and emotionally charged performances. His only completed studio album, Grace (1994), showcased his blend of rock, folk, and soul influences, earning critical acclaim and a devoted following before his untimely death at age 30.

Benson Knows Best

George Benson is a Grammy-winning guitarist and singer known for his smooth blend of jazz, R&B, and pop. Rising to fame in the 1970s with hits like “Breezin’” and “Give Me the Night,” his virtuosic guitar playing and soulful vocals made him a standout figure in contemporary jazz and crossover music.

The Men We Trust

Men I Trust is a Canadian indie pop band known for their dreamy, synth-rich sound and laid-back, nostalgic vibe. Formed in 2014, the group gained international recognition through self-released music that blends elements of chillwave, soft rock, and electronic pop, anchored by Emma Proulx’s ethereal vocals.

Where is Jazz at?

Guitar jazz is a genre that showcases the guitar as a central instrument in jazz music, blending improvisation, complex harmonies, and rhythmic sophistication. Pioneered by legends like Wes Montgomery, Django Reinhardt, and later George Benson and Pat Metheny, jazz guitar spans a range of styles from swing and bebop to fusion and smooth jazz, offering both technical mastery and expressive depth.

Strats vs Tele

The Stratocaster (Strat) and Telecaster (Tele) are two iconic electric guitars made by Fender, each with distinct features and tones. The Strat has a contoured body, three pickups, and a tremolo (whammy) bar, offering a smoother, more versatile sound often used in blues, rock, and funk. The Tele, with its simpler, slab body and two pickups, delivers a bright, punchy tone known for its twang—perfect for country, indie rock, and classic rhythm playing. Both are legendary, but they feel and sound quite different in the hands..

The Instagram Guitarists Dillemma

The "Instagram guitarist's dilemma" refers to the tension many modern players face between showcasing technical skill for social media clout and developing as well-rounded, expressive musicians. While platforms like Instagram reward short, flashy clips and speed-driven playing, this can sometimes come at the expense of musicality, songwriting, and deeper artistic growth—leaving guitarists torn between gaining online visibility and cultivating long-term creative substance.

Hybrid!

Charlie Hunter is a groundbreaking guitarist known for his use of custom seven- and eight-string hybrid guitars that let him play bass lines, chords, and melodies simultaneously. His instruments are designed with separate bass and guitar strings, allowing him to cover multiple musical roles at once—often sounding like an entire rhythm section by himself. Emerging in the 1990s, Hunter quickly gained attention for his blend of jazz, funk, blues, and groove-based improvisation, delivered with tight rhythmic precision and deep musicality. His innovative technique and musical versatility have redefined the possibilities of the guitar and influenced a generation of genre-crossing players.

Rythym vs Lead

Corey Wong has made significant leaps in redefining the role of rhythm guitar, turning what’s often a background element into a dynamic, front-and-center voice. Known for his ultra-tight, funk-infused playing with bands like Vulfpeck and in his solo work, Wong blends precision, groove, and clean tone to create rhythm parts that are as expressive and engaging as solos. His energetic stage presence, sharp rhythmic phrasing, and creative use of space have helped elevate rhythm guitar to a lead role in modern funk, pop, and instrumental music.

Stay Motivated

Staying motivated on guitar can be tough, but every great player once struggled through the same chords, scales, and plateaus you're facing now. Progress might feel slow some days, but every minute you spend playing adds up—so trust the process, celebrate small wins, and remember why you picked up the guitar in the first place: for the joy of creating music. Keep going—you’re building something that truly lasts.
